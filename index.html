How your water source and daily intake may affect exposure to “forever chemicals”

PFAS are often called “forever chemicals” because many of them do not break down easily in the environment or the body. If PFAS are present in drinking water, higher daily water intake may increase relative exposure.

This is a simplified educational estimate. It does not measure actual PFAS levels or diagnose health risk.

    Universal Tips

    • Check your local water quality report and PFAS testing updates.

    • Stay informed about state and federal PFAS policy changes.

    What Can You Do Beyond Your Own Home?

    • Ask your water utility about PFAS testing frequency and findings.
